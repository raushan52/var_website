Understanding User Experience (UX) Design

We will all agree that experience is a teacher of all things and the only source of knowledge is experience. Every activity is an experience by itself. Staying with user experience, many organizations into Information Technology and Software developers in particular would be no strangers, to UX Design. Irrespective of any sector and utility, a product is designed with one objective to be achieved and that is User Experience. Technology is no exception to that. To put it in a simple way, User Experience (UX) design is the process of designing a product that gives an ultimate user experience. To offer the end user, a meaningful and relevant experience, UX design process includes product development, brand building, functionality and application.

We have heard about this phrase many times and still continue to hear it; “Experience makes any man perfect”. The User Experience Designer’s job description too revolves around more of brain work and strategizing. The field of UX design is a multi-faceted field. Creating a product which satisfies the end user is not only a challenging task, but a learning experience for many budding UX designers, in its own way as they obviously get all excited when they see a requirement which says, “UX Designers for Hire!”. However, before companies hire a UX Designer, there are few of the basics that they look to as a quality, inside every aspiring UX designers. They are,

Ability to know the target audience

Ability to promote and market your skills as a UX designer

Ability to conduct, surveys, tests and evaluations

Ability to translate goals and objectives into worthy experiences.

Ability to contribute within a team.
